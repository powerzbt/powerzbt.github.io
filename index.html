    Exploring An Infinitely Large Napkin

    Recently, I had the pleasure of delving into "An Infinitely Large Napkin," a recommendation from my brilliant friend CJY. This captivating book delves into an array of mathematical topics, ranging from topology to quantum mechanics, differential geometry, measure theory, and complex variable functions. What sets "An Infinitely Large Napkin" apart from other resources is its ability to provide a strong foundation and a deep understanding of various mathematical concepts. Despite not being as comprehensive as a full semester-long course, the book does an excellent job of explaining important theorems and derivations in various math majors. As someone who has always been curious about these subjects, I found the book to be a valuable resource for deepening my knowledge and satisfying my inquisitive nature. One of the most intriguing concepts I discovered while reading the book was how the state of quantum can be expressed using linear algebra and eigenvalues. This was a groundbreaking discovery for me and revolutionized the way I view quantum mechanics. I was also fascinated to learn about Shor's algorithm, which enables fast decomposition of prime factors at a theoretical level. The complexity of the topic was one that I had previously struggled to understand, but thanks to the clarity provided in the book, I was able to grasp it much better. The topological questions that CJY gave me to work on were also a highlight of my experience with the book. They were challenging, engaging, and allowed me to apply the knowledge I had gained from the book to real-world problems. This provided me with an intellectually stimulating and rewarding experience. Beyond reading the book, I also explored programming languages such as Haskell and Agda. These allowed me to verify mathematical proofs and apply the theoretical knowledge I had gained in practical settings. It was an exciting and innovative way to deepen my understanding of the subject and develop practical skills useful in various fields. I am immensely grateful to CJY for introducing me to "The Napkin," a valuable and enriching resource. The book has provided me with a deeper understanding of key mathematical concepts and challenged me to think more critically about the subject. I look forward to continuing my exploration of this fascinating world and seeing where my journey takes me next.
